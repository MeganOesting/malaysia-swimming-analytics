Interpretation

  • 24.4% of 15→16 dropouts reappear: Nearly one-third of swimmers who fall out of the top 500 at age 16 regain elite status by age 17 or 18.
  • 13.0% of 16→17 dropouts reappear: About one-fifth of swimmers who drop out at 17 return by 18.
  • Implications: Temporary setbacks (injury, training changes, motivation) are common, but most dropouts (~70%) do not return to top-500 status.
  • MOT Consideration: Delta calculations may reflect 'survivor bias' - swimmers who maintain top-500 status consistently show different improvement patterns than those who drop out.

What This Means

  • The 'Good But Not Great' Dilemma: Swimmers in the 1.5-2.0 z-score range (top 6.7%) face unique challenges - they're faster than most, but may be hitting their genetic/training ceiling.
  • Diminishing Returns: These swimmers may have already extracted most available improvement from training. Further gains require exceptional dedication or genetic advantages.
  • MOT Implication: Consider tiered MOT standards - swimmers in this range may need more modest improvement targets than those at lower performance levels.
