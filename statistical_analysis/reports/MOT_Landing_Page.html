üìä What is MOT?

Malaysia On Track (MOT) provides age-specific performance standards that help identify swimmers who are "on track" for competitive success. These standards are based on comprehensive statistical analysis of improvement patterns from top-performing developmental swimmers worldwide.

Key Features:

  • Age-Specific Benchmarks: Standards for ages 15, 16, 17, and 18 years
  • Three Development Tracks: Early, Middle, and Late developers, recognizing that swimmers develop at different rates
  • Evidence-Based: Based on the top 500 ranked swimmers from USA Swimming's database for each event, gender, and age (15-18) for each season from September 2021 to September 2025
  • Improvement Analysis: 84 separate analyses tracking how swimmers improve between ages (14 events √ó 2 genders √ó 3 age transitions: 15‚Üí16, 16‚Üí17, 17‚Üí18)
  • Internationally Validated: Compared and validated against Canada On Track standards

üîç How Are MOT Standards Created?

MOT standards are not arbitrary goals‚Äîthey are based on rigorous statistical analysis of how competitive developmental swimmers actually improve over time. Here's how we determine these standards:

1

Data Collection

  • Top 500 ranked swimmers from USA Swimming's database for each combination of:
    • 14 events (50 Free, 100 Free, 200 Free, etc.)
    • 2 genders (Female, Male)
    • 4 ages (15, 16, 17, 18 years)
    • 4 seasons (Sept 2021-Aug 2022, Sept 2022-Aug 2023, Sept 2023-Aug 2024, Sept 2024-Aug 2025)
  • This means we pull the top 500 results from USA Swimming's season rankings for each specific event, gender, and age for each season
  • This gives us approximately 112,000 individual swimmer records in our source data (14 events √ó 2 genders √ó 4 ages √ó ~500 swimmers √ó 4 seasons)
  • Ages are based on the swimmer's age on the first day of competition
2

Improvement Analysis

  • Perform 84 separate improvement analyses:
    • 14 events √ó 2 genders √ó 3 age transitions = 84 improvement analyses
    • Each improvement analysis tracks how swimmers improve between ages (15‚Üí16, 16‚Üí17, 17‚Üí18)
    • For each improvement analysis, we find swimmers who competed in both the "from" age and the "to" age groups
  • For each improvement analysis, calculate statistical measures:
    • Median improvement (the "typical" improvement)
    • Mean, standard deviation, and quartiles
    • Sample sizes (number of swimmers who appear in both age groups for that transition)
  • Note: Sample sizes in improvement analyses are typically 250-350 swimmers (fewer than 500) because not all swimmers compete in consecutive age groups
  • Identify patterns across different performance levels and events
3

Standard Setting

  • Compare USA improvement patterns with Canada On Track benchmarks
  • Establish three tracks (Early, Middle, Late developers)
  • Set age-specific time standards for each track
4

Validation

  • Cross-reference with international standards
  • Validate against competitive performance data
  • Ensure standards are achievable yet challenging
