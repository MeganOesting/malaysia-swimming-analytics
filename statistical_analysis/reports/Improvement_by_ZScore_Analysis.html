Swimmers with z-scores between 1.5 and 2.0 (top 6.7% of performers) show a strong pattern:

  • At age 15→16: 96% of transitions show positive improvement - nearly all elite swimmers improve!

  • At age 17→18: Only 43% show positive improvement - elite swimmers start plateauing

Implication for MOT: Swimmers in this z-score range (already performing in the top 6.7%) are most likely to continue improving, especially at younger ages. This validates targeting elite performers (like Canada On Track does) rather than the median population for identifying "on track" athletes.

Interpretation and Implications

What This Analysis Tells Us

  • Z-Score Range: A z-score indicates how many standard deviations above or below the mean a swimmer's time is. For swim times (lower is better), a positive z-score means faster than average.
  • Positive Correlation: If higher z-scores correlate with greater improvement, it suggests elite performers continue to improve at a higher rate than the general population.
  • Top Performers Improving: High percentages of improvement in top z-score ranges validate the Canada On Track methodology, which targets elite performers on medal trajectories.
  • Negative Deltas in Lower Ranges: Lower-performing swimmers (negative z-scores) may show negative medians, reflecting plateauing or regression in the general population.

Validation of Canada On Track Approach

If top performers (z ≥ 1.5) consistently show positive improvement rates while the median population shows negative or minimal improvement, this validates using elite performer trajectories (like Canada On Track) rather than population medians for setting Malaysia On Track (MOT) targets.

Understanding Weak Positive Correlation (1.2%)

A correlation of +1.2% (0.012) between z-score and improvement means there is almost no relationship between how fast a swimmer is at one age and how much they'll improve the next year. This is actually significant:

  • It means both fast and slow swimmers improve (or don't improve) at roughly the same rate
  • However, the 1.5-2.0 z-score range pattern shows that elite swimmers (top 6.7%) are more consistent in their improvement, especially at younger ages
  • This suggests that while improvement amount may not correlate strongly with starting performance level, improvement consistency does - elite swimmers are more likely to continue improving year-over-year
