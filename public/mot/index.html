Key Assumptions & Characteristics:

  • Elite Sample Only: We have the top 500 swimmers per event/gender/age, representing approximately 7-12% of all competitive swimmers for popular events.
  • Distribution Shape: The full population would be highly right-skewed (many slower swimmers, few elite swimmers). Our sample is left-truncated (top 500 US Swimmers only), which is appropriate for setting the MOT times designed to identify swimmers on track for elite performance.

Understanding the Canadian Data

Canada On Track Times - Swimming Canada's athlete identification methodology

The purpose of the "On Track Times", used exclusively in the Olympic program stream, is to help identify a pool of athletes that Swimming Canada can work with in order to increase the likelihood that it is directing resources to those athletes who have the highest probability of positively impacting Canada's international senior swimming results.

How the Tracking Times Were Determined:

Swimming Canada introduced On Track times in 2013 as a tool to better identify developing swimmers. Performance data from international competitions and average progression rates of Canadian age group swimmers were used to develop a series of three tracks for each Olympic event. The three tracks were meant to capture everyone from early to late developers who had the potential to represent Canada internationally.

Since spring 2014, Swimming Canada has worked closely with a sports analytics group established by Canadian Tire Financial Services in collaboration with Own The Podium. The analysts worked with more than two million sets of results from world-class athletes to help provide insight into their career progression.

Key Evolution:

  • Version 2 (2017): Continued as a tool to identify developing swimmers tracking towards world-class performance, maintaining the original principles.
  • Current Version: Published annually following each long course World Championships, incorporating the latest World Aquatics A standards as the final On Track time.
  • Seamless Pathway: By utilizing World Aquatics A standards, it provides a more seamless pathway from initial athlete identification to senior team qualification.
